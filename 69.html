SUPER CLASS: TETRAPODA -
4.2.11.4 Class-1: Amphibia
It is a class of terrestrial tetrapods which lead a dual life in water as well as on moist land as the skin is
devoid of protective scales and the larval stage is aquatic.
The animal possesses two pairs of jointed pentadactyl limb. Their digits do not possess claws, nails, or hoofs.
Body divisible into head and trunk.
Skin moist, without scales.
Tympanum represents ear.
Cloaca
is the common chamber where alimentary, urinary and reproductive tracts open.
