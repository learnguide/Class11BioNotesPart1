Fig. 11. Different stages of meiosis II
In
metaphase II
, the centromeres are present with two kinetochores that are attached to spindle fibers from the
centrosomes at opposite poles. The metaphasic plate is rotated by 90 degrees when compared to meiosis I,
perpendicular to the previous plate.
Anaphase II
is accompanied by sister chromatid segregation. The remaining cohesin is degraded to allow sister
chromatid segregation.
Telophase II
, which is like telophase I, de-condensation of chromosomes occurs. Nuclear envelopes reorganize
and cleavage or cell plate formation starts to produce four daughter cells, each with a haploid set of chromosomes.
Significance of Meiosis:
•
Maintenance of chromosome number generation after generation in case of sexual reproduction.
•
Increases variation in population.
