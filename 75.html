Fibrous root:
It consists of thin, moderately branching roots growing from the base of the stem. The
primary root is not prominent and is replaced by a large number of roots. The fibrous root systems
appears as a thick tuft of roots forming a mat-like structure at maturity. It is universal in
monocotyledonous plants and ferns.
