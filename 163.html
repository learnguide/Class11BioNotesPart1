Cellulose
is an unbranched polymer of glucose units.
It is a structural polysaccharide of plant cells. It is the most
abundant organic molecule in the biosphere. It is found in plant cells and provide strength and rigidity to the cell.

Chitin
is a linear polysaccharide composed of N-acetyl-D-glucosamine residues. It is found in the exoskeleton of
insects and crustaceans.
Reducing and non-reducing sugar
Sugars capable of reducing ferric or cupric ion are called reducing sugar. All monosaccharides are reducing
sugars.
Non-reducing sugars are not capable of reducing ferric or cupric ion. For example, sucrose is a non-reducing
sugar.
Lipids
They are insoluble or poorly insoluble in water. They are soluble in non-polar solvents such as ether, chloroform
or benzene.
