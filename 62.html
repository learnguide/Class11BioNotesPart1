Classification
Class I: Polyplacophora- shell with 8 pieces. Eg:
Chilton
Class II: Monoplacophora- shell with a single piece. Eg:
Neopilina
Class III: Aplacophora-shell absent. Eg:
Neomenia
Class IV: Scaphopoda-shell tubulr. Eg:
Dentalium
Class V: Pelecypoda- shell with 2 pieces. Eg : Mussel, Oyster, Teredo, etc.
Class VI: Gastropoda- shell with one piece and spirally coiled. Eg : Pila, Snail, Slug etc.
Class VII: Cephalopoda- shell internal or reduced. Eg: Loligo, Cuttle fish, Devil fish, etc.
4.2.9 Phylum Echinodermata
(Echinos-spines; derm-skin)
They are spiny skinned or spiny bodied animals.
The term echinodermata was introduced by
J.Klein
.
Important characteristic features:
•
They are exclusively
marine
animals.
•
The body is covered by large number of
spines
which are generally immovable and protective in
function, but in some, the spines are movable and help in locomotion. Few spines also get modified
to form organs of defence known as pedicellaria. Eg: Sea urchin, Star fish, etc.
•
They possess an
organ system level
of body organisation. They are
triploblastic
animals.
