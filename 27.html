3.1.3 RHODOPHYCEAEA (Red Algae):
Commonly called as red algae.
They are mostly marine and rarely fresh water.
Eg:
Betrachospermum
.
They occur in well lighted region and also in depths of oceans.
The thallus is multicellular.
Pigments present are chlorophyll a, d and phycoerythrin.
Red colour is due to the presence of red pigment called
r-phycoerythrin.
Food is stored in the form of
floridean starch which is similar to amylopectin and glycogen
in structure.
Vegetative reproduction by fragmentation
Asexual by nonmotile spores
Sexual by Oogamy and has complex post fertilization developments.
The common red algae are
Polysiphonia, Porphyra, Gracilaria, Gelidium, Betrachospermum
etc.
