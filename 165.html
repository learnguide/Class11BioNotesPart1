Co-factor:
Non-protein constituents that are bound to the enzyme and make the enzyme catalytically
active.
•
Co-enzyme:
Organic compounds that bind to the enzyme transiently during the course of the reaction.
•
Prosthetic groups:
Organic substances that are bound very tightly to the enzyme.
Factors affecting the enzyme activity
Temperature, pH and substrate concentration affects the enzyme activity. High or low temperature inactivates the
enzyme activity. Deprotonation or protonation alters the enzyme activity.
Enzyme can also be inactivated by molecules known as inhibitors. The most common type of inhibitors are
competitive inhibitors. These inhibitors compete with substrate for binding to the enzyme active site. For example,
inhibition of succinate dehydrogenase by Malonate.
